Layers

In System Book, there are 4 main layers (strategic level) and 2 auxiliary layers so that themes can be represented at different levels and theme details can be conveyed to the user with a specific semantic template.

Master Layers

A. Core: The Core, in which the core competencies of the UNYT are defined, its main strategy is specified, its competitive structure is designed, its difference from other units of the university is revealed, and the future of the structure is discussed, constitutes the focus of the System Book architecture.

B. Policies Practices and bases on all Core-related issues are available in the Policies section of the relevant theme. The minimum requirements and needs in transforming policies into practice and activity are defined in this layer.

C. Processes: Guiding principles, way of doing business, recommendations and best practices that form the basis of practices carried out with certain standards are included in this section. The chapter also includes detailed descriptions of process performance and success criteria.

D. Prosedures: The basic issues needed to carry out an application, activity or process are explained in this section, including the sequence of work.

Auxiliary Layers

E. Flowcharts: This is the section where practical materials (visual tools, etc.) regarding the definitions and explanations in the Procedures section can be found, and quick and detailed information about the processes can be obtained.

F. Support Documents: This is the section where the documents that will be needed are located.
